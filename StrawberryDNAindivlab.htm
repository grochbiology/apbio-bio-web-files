Strawberry DNA Extraction  

 

Centrifuge Tube

Test tube rack

Zip-lock sandwich bag

Paper clip or wooden stick

Coffee filter in a beaker

95-99% ice cold ethanol or isopropyl alcohol

Whole strawberry (or any fruit or vegetable fresh or frozen)

Lysis buffer: (dish detergent (10 ml joy works well or clear shampoo + ¼ tsp salt + 90 ml distilled water) *Note if using meat:  Add unseasoned meat tenderizer to the lysis buffer.

 

Procedure:

  1. If the fruit or vegetable has leaves, remove them
  2. Place the fruit or vegetable into the zip-lock sandwich bag.  Seal the bag and pulverize the fruit/vegetable.  Smash the fruit/vegetable by hand and then roll a pen or marker back and forth over the bag to make the fruit/vegetable as liquid as possible.
  3. Add 10 ml of the lysis buffer to the bag and reseal.
  4. Continue to roll the fruit tissue in the lysis buffer for 2 minutes. Caution: don’t make really bubbly!
  5. Place a coffee filter in the top of a 6 oz cup or the cheese cloth over a 6 oz cup.
  6. Cut off the end corner of the baggie and pour the contents of the sandwich bag into the filter and set aside for 10 minutes to drain the liquid into the cup.
  7. Discard the coffee filter and its contents.
  8. Pour 10 ml of ice cold alcohol into a centrifuge tube.
  9. Pour 5ml of the contents of the cup into the centrifuge tube.  Cap the tube and wait for the DNA  to start to precipitate out into the alcohol.  (The process begins almost immediately and the DNA will continue to condense for the next few minutes.  Wait at least 5 minutes for the precipitation to complete.
  10. Use the wooden stick (or a paper clip formed into a hook) to gently lift some of the precipitated DNA to observe it’s characteristics.  
  11. The DNA can now be used for further biotechnological studies (PCR, Restriction enzyme digestion etc…)

You can take your tube of DNA home to show it to your parents.
