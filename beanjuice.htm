Biology:   Bean Juice Amylase Lab

 

Problem: What affects the efficiency of bean amylase?

 

Pre-lab: use this link Pre-lab

 

Materials:

small beakers, plastic syringes, hot plate, distilled water, dropper bottles with Lugol's solution (IKI), 1M Hydrochloric Acid- located on the front table, bean juice (water that dried beans soaked in overnight), starch solution

 

Procedure:

1.  Measure out 10 ml of the starch solution with the assigned syringe and place in a beaker.

2.  Add 3 drops of IKI to the beaker.  Note color change in your journal.

3.  Fill a different syringe with bean juice.  Add one drop at a time of bean juice to the starch-IKI solution until the solution clears.  Note how many milliliters it takes to clear the solution. (Trial 1)

4.  Clean your beaker.  Follow steps 1-3 again, this time adding 1 drops of 1M HCl (hydrochloric acid) to the starch solution in beaker in step 1.

(trial 2)

5.  Clean your beaker. Add 10 ml of starch, this time put your beaker on the hot plate set to the lowest temperature.  Heat only until steam starts to form. Remove the beaker from the hot plate; add your IKI, etc. (Trial 3)

6.  Clean your beaker.  Add 5 ml of starch solution plus 5 ml of distilled water to the beaker. Add 3 drops of IKI.  Repeat instruction #3. (Trial 4)

7. Obtain 10 ml of starch from the front of the room that is in the ice bucket, note the temperature of the thermometer, add this starch to a clean beaker, Add 1 drop of IKI, Repeat instruction #3. (Trial 5)

8. Table choice, add a salt solution (3 drops of 5% NaCl) or 3 drops of acid or 3 drops of a base to the 10 mL of starch. Add 1 drop of IKI. Repeat step 3.
