Energy Transfer and Biochemical Reactions: Directions: Work in your lab groups to answer the following questions, make sure each member of the group understands all the concepts. Any figures that are mentioned are found in the 8th Edition.

 

1.      Explain how mitochondria and chloroplasts are related to proteobacteria? What is the name of the theory that describes how these organelles became part of eukaryotic cells?

2.      What structures are common to both organelles, which structures differ?

3.      How does the structure of ATP support its function (ability) as a molecule capable of storing and releasing energy?

4.      What is the major difference between anaerobic and aerobic respiration?

5.      What is notable about the names of the enzymes involved in both cellular respiration and photosynthesis? Are there any suffices that consistently appear in the enzymes names, what are their functions?

6.      Which parts of a glucose molecule are actually relevant to the recharging of ATP? Which parts of the glucose molecule are released?

7.      What is the difference between substrate level phosphorylation and oxidative phosphorylation? Which process yielded the maximum amount of recharged ATP?

8.      Trace the movement of electrons (e-) and hydrogen ions (H+) from glycolysis through chemiosmosis in mitochondria.

9.      Why are the processes listed above considered energy transfer? How do these processes relate to the 1st law of thermodynamics?

10.  Which forms (types) of energy are involved in photosynthesis?

11.  Why does photosynthesis appear to be the antithesis of cellular respiration? What concepts and materials support the above statement? Refutes the above statement?

12.  Trace the movement of elections and hydrogen ions in photosynthesis? Any processes that seem similar to cellular respiration? Are the similarities related to similar functions?
