Plant Unit Study Guide

Reading Ch 22-1, Ch 23-1, 23-4 and 23-5, Ch 25, journal notes on plant specimens

22 multiple choice, 1 drawing and 10 plant identifications (either Phylum, type (gymnosperm, angiosperm, monocot, dicot) or common name)

1. Define a plant.

2. Why do plants need light?

3. What are the characteristics of Bryophytes? What do they need that the seed plants do not to reproduce?

4. What is are the components of vascular tissue? What are the functions of each component?

5. How are seed-bearing plants different from the other types of plants?

6. Compare and contrast an angiosperm monocot from an angiosperm dicot. (petal numbers, seed part numbers, etc)

7. What are the major parts of a seed plant?

8. What are the structures you would find in a cross-section of a dicot leaf? What are the functions of each structure? Be able to identify all the different parts of the cross-section of a leaf.

9. Why are stomata necessary?

10. What is the function of auxin, ethylene gas, gibberellin in a plant's life?

11. Name four types of tropisms. What are the purposes of these tropisms and how do they help plants?

12. What is dormancy?

13. Where do angiosperms produce their seeds?

14. Which chemicals do plants use tor espond to changes in their environment?

15. Which substances produced in one part of a plant affect another part of the plant?

16. Which group of plants first colonized the Earth's surface? Second? Third? Fourth?

17. Know the structure of a dicot leaf.

18. Study the specimens that you looked at in the lab. Know their Division/Phylum, type/class (gymnosperm, angiosperm (monocot or dicot) and common name).Leaf Lab Specimen List
