Lab Behavior and Safety Rules

 

Laboratory exercises and hands-on activities will be used as much as possible to demonstrate the concepts presented in this class.  Science is a hands-on subject and the experimentation we do in this class will exemplify the types of studies scientists conduct in the real world.  Lab work is a great way to have fun while exploring biological concepts, however proper behavior and adherence to rules is imperative for both the learning process and the safety of the participants. Here are some basic guidelines to follow during laboratory time; certain assignments may have more precautions based on the nature of the experiment.

 

1)      Pay attention!  Always pay attention to the rules listed for each experiment and carefully observe them as you proceed. The pre-lab discussion may introduce important safety information. Never leave an experiment unattended! "An ounce of prevention is worth a pound of cure".

2)   A clean lab space is a safe lab space! Keep all books, paper and other flammable materials away from open flames or other dangerous chemicals. Immediately clean any spills and dirty instruments. This will promote safety and limit contamination in your experiments. Never leave any materials in the sinks. Return all materials to their appropriate place at the end of lab. Discard all waste matter in the appropriate containers (example: broken glass in glass pail).

3)    Proper wardrobe! Do not were baggy shirts in lab that may get in your way, knock things over, etc. Lab coats are optional, but recommended. Always wear protective eyewear. Always tie long hair back.

4)    Respect chemicals! Never mix chemicals you are not instructed to or otherwise perform unscheduled experiments. Never taste, smell, or touch chemicals or use chemicals from unmarked containers unless directed to by the teacher. Never return excess chemicals back to their container. Keep volatile substances away from gas burners or other heating sources.

5)    Use precaution with glassware! Point the open end of a test tube or flask away from yourself and others when heating it. When inserting the glass tube into a stopper, use glycerine or water to lubricate.

6)    Fire is dangerous! Turn all gas burners or other heat sources off when they are not in use. Be cautious of hot materials and do not handle them with bare hands.

7)    Hygiene is important! Always wash hands throughout an experiment or dissection. Never put pens, pencils or other materials in your mouth!

8)  No goofing off! Most lab accidents are easily prevented by paying attention and not goofing around with classmates. Use all materials in lab only for their intended purpose. Misbehavior during lab will result in a zero for that lab.  Severe misbehavior during lab will result in a zero and in an hour of detention.  If poor lab behavior continues, more severe measures will be taken. 

9)    Stay calm! If an emergency situation does arrive, remain calm. Be sure you know where all the lab safety equipment is located (fire extinguisher, eye wash area, etc.) If you see a classmate in trouble, tell them what to do an assist them in doing it.

 

If a lab is missed, an alternative assignment will be given; you have two days to turn this in. Only two labs per term may be made up for in this manner.
