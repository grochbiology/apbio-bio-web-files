Living or Not Living Lab Activity

This lab will require 3-4 students. You will need yeast, sand, sugar, a balance, a stop watch, and pen/pencil.

Procedure:

1). Place a few dry yeast grains in a microscope slide. (Be careful not to blow the grains off). Observe yeast under the microscope on low power

2). Draw a picture of what you see in your journal see the Microscope Rules .

3). Record any signs of "life".

4). Using a new microscope slide, repeat steps 1-3 using a few grains of sand.

5) Tare  the mass of a Petri dish.

6). Add a small amount of dry yeast (> 0.5 grams) to the Petri dish; record the mass of the yeast.

7). Repeat steps 5 and 6 (use a new Petri dish) with sand.

8) Weigh between  5-10 grams of sugar, add to a beaker containing 60 mL of warm tap water. Record actual mass of sugar.

9) Carefully add 10 mL of sugar water to the sand and yeast containing Petri dishes.

10). Observe signs of "life".

Observations:

1) Did anything happen in either Petri dish? If so, which one(s)? List all of your observations.

2) Why do you think you observed what you did see, or why do you think nothing happened?
