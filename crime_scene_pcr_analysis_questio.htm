Crime Scene PCR Analysis Questions

(from Bio-Rad and http://www.lakesideschool.org/upperschool/departments/science/forensics/documents/Lab6-DNAfingerprinting_000.doc )

 

1. What is the genotype of each of your samples? If your samples did not generate PCR products, explain why that may have happened?

 

2. Summarize the results of your gel; does the Crime Scene DNA sample have a genotype that matches any of the suspects? If so, which one matches? How conclusive would this be in a court of law (i.e. how definitive is this in terms of linking a suspect to a crime scene or implicating guilt)? Would the results of this analysis be sufficient to say with authority that the suspect whose DNA matched that of the crime scene committed the crime? Why or why not?

 

3. What do your results tell you about which suspects should be included in the investigation? Excluded? Explain your answer.

 

4. Imagine that each allele at the BXP007 locus is found at exactly the same frequency in a population. Since there are 8 possible alleles at the BXP007 locus, what is the frequency of any one allele from this locus in this population?

 

5. Related to question 2 and question 4, if you had a pool of 13 suspects, and only one suspect had a genotype that matched the BXP007 locus found at the crime scene, would you be satisfied that you had identified the perpetrator based only on the genotype frequency calculated for the BXP007 locus? Why or why not? Explain your answer.

 

6. Case Study. Find at least one case where DNA fingerprinting played a key role in the conviction of a criminal (besides those discussed in your text or in the case studies you reviewed in this class). Write a brief synopsis of the crime(s) committed (including names and dates where relevant), how DNA fingerprinting was used by forensic scientists (and ultimately the prosecution) and briefly summarize the court proceedings and the judgment. Finally, based on your experience with and research on DNA profiling (particularly STRs), do you feel this evidence should be admissible under the Federal Rules of Evidence? Should it be admissible in some cases and not others? How many loci should be surveyed to provide a conclusive test (you may need to do some research to see what states and countries require in terms of number of loci)? Be sure and cite all sources used, including books and websites, and definitely provide URLs for pictures that you might use.
