Leaf Identification Lab Specimens (2007)

Key prefixes and suffices: phyta = plant; sperm = seed; bryos = moss; pterido = wing(fern); pino = pine; gymno = naked; angio = covered

Domain Eukarya

Kingdom Plantae

Division (same as Phylum)

 Division: Bryophyta (moss plants) still need water to reproduce; have sperm with flagella, no vascular system (no phloem or xylem), no real leaves, roots or stems (leaflets and rootlets)  Moss
Division Pteridophyta (fern plants) earliest tracheophyte (has vascular system), still needs water to reproduce, have sperm with flagella Fern
   
Superdivision: Spermatophyta (seed plants) true land plants; no sperm use pollen  
Division: Pinophyta (also know as coniferophyta)

Class: Gymnosperm (naked seeds)

 Monterey Pine, Coastal Redwood, Noble fir
Division: Magnoliophyta (like the magnolia plant) also known as Angiosperms (flowering plants  
Class: Magnoliopsida commonly known as Dicotyledons (Dicots)

Have leaves with a midrib and branching veins, 2 seed leaves, sprouts 2 leaves, has flower petals in multiples of 4 and 5

 Magnolia, California fuschia flowering gooseberry, margarite, rosemary, sage, mock orange, aspen, white alder, Quercus agrifolia (coast live oak), eastern oak, crab apple, pear tree blossoms, oleander, camillia
Class Liliopsida commonly know as Monocotyledons (Monocots)

Have leaves with parallel veins running up to the tip of the blade, one seed part, sprouts 1 leaf, flower petals in multiples of 3

 Blue grass, fortnight lily, bearded lily (all leaf blades no flowers), yucca blade
