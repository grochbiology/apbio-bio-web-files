Honors (Accelerated) Biology Course Description and Policies 2003/2004

 

COURSE DESCRIPTION: This course is for the highest achieving Freshman or Sophomore student who is considering taking Advanced Placement Biology as a Junior or Senior. Each unit of study will involve inquiry and or problem based laboratory exercises. There will be one to two required independent research projects per semester. One of these projects must be used to expand into a local, regional, or state science fair project. Students will have access to advanced laboratory techniques. Students will keep a research journal, utilize technology in their lab reports. Students will be expected to complete a summer assignment. This course will meet the U.C. and C.S.U. laboratory science requirements. A grade of C or better is needed to fulfill this requirement. This is NOT a weighted course, as Universities do not recognize weighted courses for freshmen students.

 Students will study the major themes of biology utilizing a variety of inquiry-based laboratories, and lessons. The major themes include: Evolution: patterns and products of change; Interaction and Interdependence; Genetic continuity and reproduction; Growth, development, and differentiation; Energy, matter and organization; Maintenance of a dynamic equilibrium; Science, technology and society; Science as a process.

ASSIGNMENTS:

Homework: All homework is to be typed single spaced #10 font, and corrected with a red ink pen during class discussions. It must be clearly marked with the Chapter, Question (s) #, DUE DATE, and this must be highlighted. All graded/recorded/returned assignments are to be kept in the student binder. All assignments are due on the date assigned. You are allowed one 'oops' late assignment per semester. Any 'oops' assignments are due as soon as possible and no later than the unit test.

Labs: Lab notes, diagrams, and data are to be written in the lab journal in pencil. Begin a new page for each lab.  Lab title and date must be written and highlighted on the first line of the page. Lab procedure packets are to be kept in the binder in a special section.

Post lab analysis/questions are to be typed, unless a specific handout is assigned w/ spaces to fill in the answers. All lab abstracts and lab reports are to be typed single space, #10 font.

Journal Entries: Entries include all class notes, daily questions and video notes. Topic (e.g. Lecture: photosynthesis), and date must be written at the top of the page and highlighted. Begin a new page in your journal when a new topic is introduced.

Absences and Tardiness: Only those with legal absences have the right to make-up work for credit. You have three days by district policy to make up missed assignments, or by arrangement, please see me if you need to make these arrangements. . It is the responsibility of the student to check the calendar for missed assignments, pick up any handouts, seek clarification if confused and to make up missed laboratory work, tests and quizzes at lunch or after school by arrangement within one week or no credit will be given.

Tardiness is a disruption. Excessive tardiness* will lead to class or school detention. Three illegal absences* may cause you to be dropped from this course. See student handbook for details. (* See Academic Responsibility and Accountability section).(Academic Responsibility Information)

Academic Responsibility/Cheating Policy: Please read the student guide for specific consequences. Cheating, which includes plagiarism, is defined as representing any work that is not your own original work as your work. This includes using another student's work from a previous year, this year, looking at another student's test, work copied and pasted from the internet or other published material, etc. All work turned in must be in your own words. There are no joint assignments. You may consult with other students, but your work must be original.

Daily Class Procedures: Class starts when the bell rings. Your homework must be turned in before the bell, (if called for on the board or overhead at the beginning of class) or it will be considered late, and only worth 50% of the total possible points. Always check the front board for daily instructions, homework assignments and activities.

Extra Credit: Extra credit may be offered periodically during the semester. These opportunities will be announced in class and generally posted in the classroom. Extra credit points cannot exceed 3% of the total possible points.

 

Success Tips:

1. Attend class daily. Turn in all work on time, neat and legible.

2. Take pride in your work. Make every effort to do all of the assignments, especially for days you have missed. Even though you receive reduced points for late work you are still responsible for the subject matter covered. It will help you on the Final exam in the long run.

3. Participate in class discussions: ask questions; take notes; get notes when absent.

4. Maintain communication with your teacher by phone, fax or e-mail.

5. Cooperate and participate during labs. You will be expected to know how to use equipment and how to do certain procedures.

6. Review for quizzes and tests.
