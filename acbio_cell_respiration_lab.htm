How Does Exercise Affect Cellular Respiration in Male and Female AC Bio students?

 

Pre-Lab:

Read through lab and determine the IV, DV, 5 SV's, control.  Make a hypothesis (HA) for each trial as well as a null (H0).  Make a data table as well. 

 

Procedure:

1.       Decide on two different exercises and record them in your data table.  Tidal tests will be done to measure lung capacity.

2.     Pick one person in your group to time each trial, one person will need to have the tidal test ready, and one/two students to do the exercising.  Each exercising student will complete both a standing “control” and an alternate exercise of your choosing.  The standing control is completed first (duh...).

3.     To measure your tidal volume, follow the directions below for a peak flow meter:  (if you don't have a peak flow meter, follow the instructions for using a 9-10" balloon below)

  1. Move the marker on the peak flow meter to the bottom of the scale so that it reads zero or is at base level.
     
  2. Stand up straight.
     
  3. Take a deep breath and fill your lungs all the way.
     
  4. Hold your breath while you place the device in your mouth, and close your lips around the mouthpiece. Do not put your tongue inside the hole.
     
  5. Blow out as hard and as fast as you can for one to two seconds. You want to move the marker as far as you can.
     
  6. Write down the number you receive.
     
  7. Repeat steps one to six for a total of three times. Record the highest of the three numbers — this is your peak flow number.
     
  8. Check to see which peak flow zone your peak flow number is in.

4.     Repeat step 3 for standing control two more times and record data.

5.     Conduct your chosen activity.

6.     Immediately after exercising repeat steps 3 and 4. Record all data from both students exercising.

7.     Average the peak flow numbers for the control and exercise.  Using the averages, calculate the % change for each student's exercise.  Record in the data table.  % change = exercise peak flow number - control peak flow number/ control peak flow number X 100

 

Post-Lab:

1.       Does the data support/refute the alternate hypothesis (HA)?  Restate HA cite your data in your answer (do males and females show significant differences in tidal volume and does the class show a difference between standing vs exercise volumes). 

2.     What were two possible sources of errors (you can't say you didn't have any) and how would those errors affect the results of the experiment?

3.  What is the relationship between your tidal volume and cellular respiration?

4.       What factors should you have considered before creating means from your data? (age, height, weight, gender, athlete, etc).

5.  Do a t-test to see if there is a difference between males and females standing, and exercising. Do a t-test between the whole class standing and exercising. See Statistics PPT, include handwritten calculations.
