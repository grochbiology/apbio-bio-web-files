PICNIC SICK

 

 

It’s family reunion time! Your large, spread-out family is having their big reunion in a large national park on a major holiday weekend. Some of the families own organic farms and have traveled quite far by car to join the party. Other members of the family have traveled great distances from across the country. The cousins all decide that it would be great to go for a dip after eating in the creek.  After your swim you get some Gatorade from the table in the shade under a large tree. You and your cousins each sneak a deviled egg off the table that is loaded with all types of food. Then you lie down under another tree and talk until you all doze off.

 

That night after dinner, some of the adults break out their guitars, banjos, spoons, fiddles and tambourines for a music session. It’s well  after midnight when everyone finally turns in for the night. You are awakened well before dawn by a growing commotion outside your tent. Your older brother is not lying next to you and you crawl out to discover at least a dozen people, adults and children, doubled over in pain and moaning. Seems the family has a case of food poisoning.

 

Since your only complaint is being awakened before sunrise, your job is to determine what caused this disaster at your family reunion.

 

Product:

You and your group must provide a chart showing the activity of the picnic’s participants. Your typed "news" written report must include the probable organism, the symptoms, treatments, the immune response or other body reactions and future preventative measures. References are also required.
