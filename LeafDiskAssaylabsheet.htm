Today you will be using an assay, which will allow you to measure the relative rates of photosynthesis.  It is called a Floating Leaf Disk Assay.

 First you will use the assay to look at what effect NaHCO3 has on photosynthesis.  NaHCO3 is a source of C0­2.  Then you will design your own experiment to look at one of the variables you listed above.

LEAF DISK ASSAY

 

Materials

 

Plastic syringe

Green leaves

Buffer A (without NaHCO3)

Buffer b (with NaHCO3)

Plastic Petri dishes

Paper punch 

Fluorescent light source

 

 

Procedure

(A diagram of the set-up follows the procedure)

 

1.       Obtain several green leaves and using the paper punch, punch at least 20 leaf disks and place disks in a plastic petri dish containing Buffer A.

 

2.     Obtain a syringe and pull out the plunger.  Using forceps carefully transfer 10 leaf disks to the syringe.  Replace the plunger.

 

3.     Draw at least 8 mls of Buffer B into the syringe.  Invert the syringe and gently push the plunger to remove all the air.

 

4.     While holding your finger or thumb over the tip, pull back on the plunger.  This will create a vacuum, which will pull all the air out of the leaf disks.  The leaf disks should sink to the bottom.  If they do not "flick" the chamber a few times with your finger to dislodge the air bubbles trapped under the leaf disks.  Repeat pulling and tapping until all the disks have sunk to the bottom of the "chamber".

 

5.     Repeat this procedure using buffer A.

 

6.     Place the "chambers" about 5cm from a fluorescent light source.  Make sure the chamber is exposed to the light.  You may have to put the chambers on top of a book or other item. Begin timing when the light is turned on.  Record when the disks reach the top.

 


As photosynthesis proceeds, oxygen accumulates in the intercellular spaces in the leaves.  Therefore, this floating leaf disk assay utilizes the rate at which oxygen is produced as a measure of the rate at which photosynthesis is occurring.
