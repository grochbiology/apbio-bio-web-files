Pre-Lab:(to be completed in your journal before class)

1.  What is Bromthymol blue (BTB) solution?

2.  What color change occurs to BTB as you slowly exhale into it?

3.  Read through entire lab procedure and identify the IV, DV, and 3SV’s.

4.  Write a hypothesis for each form of exercise that will affect your body’s rate of CO2 production?

5.  Make a data table ready to record your data in during the lab activity in your journal.

6.  Label the axes and create a title for a graph in your journal.

7. Summarize the procedure.

 

Procedure:

1.       Decide on two different exercises and record them in your data table. Label each test tube according to what exercise you choose to test. Put 10ml of the BTB solution in each test tube. Everyone needs to wear goggles and an apron for safety.

2.     You will be working at a lab table in groups of tow. Pick one person in your group to time each trial, one person will need to replenish the BTB solution (dump used solution into waste beaker), and up to two students to do the exercising.

3.     Do the "standing" control first. When your timer says “go”, slowly blow air into through the straw into the bottom of the test tube. CAUTION: do not inhale through the straw.

4.     When the solution changes color, your partner should say “stop”, and then record how long (in seconds) the color change took. Repeat standing control three times.

5.     Conduct your chosen activity for one minute.

6.     Immediately repeat steps 3 and 4 using the same test tube and straw.  Repeat three times.

7.   Record all the data from both groups.

Post-Lab: (answer in your journal)

1.  Calculate the ratio differences between your control and your exercises and record in the data table.     How did exercise affect the time for the solution to change color?

2.     What process in your body produces CO2? How does exercise affect the process of cellular respiration?

3.     Compare your results with your partner. Explain why your results could have varied?

4.     Were your hypotheses supported by the data? Explain your answer citing your data.

5.     Graph your results. Don’t forget a title and to label your axes.
