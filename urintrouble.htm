U. R. in TROUBLE!

Background: The big job of the kidney is to maintain(homeostasis), chemical equilibrium, of the body, by filtering theblood that circulates through it. The Kidney converts what's leftover into urine. About 2000 L of blood pass through both kidneys aday, yet only 1.5 -2 L exit. That contains all of the liquid wastethat needs to be eliminated.

Scenario:

You are a clinical technician (lab tech). Several urine sampleshave arrived at the laboratory and the labels have become separatedfrom the samples. You have the patient's names and probablediagnosis on the labels. Design an experiment to match the unknownsamples with their owners.

The doctors have listed the following possible diseases: Anemia, Diabetes, Jaundice- Liver Damage, Kidney Damage, Bladder Infection

Laboratory Tests:

Test Indicator used to test urine Normal ranges and other information
pH pH paper normal: slightly acidic (~pH 6.8-7.0), below ph6.8 suggests acidosis, diabetes, kidney disease, heart disease, excessive diarrhea
protein Biurets detects the presence of nitrogen, sign of using protein for energy, kidney damage
glucose glucose test strips diabetes
broken blood cells color  liver disease (detected by red or brownish color) or presence of RBC kidney damage
specific gravity urinometer- or called hydrometers Detects kidney failure: above 1.025 excessive salt being excreted) (normal range is 1.010 to 1.025)
Bile shaking test-tube if present shows starvation, liver damage,detected by and seeing if urine forms bubbles
RBC: slide examination (preprepared) presence of RBC (broken glomeruli; high blood pressure, mechanical damage to kidney) or WBC (infection)
salt crystals slide examination (preprepared) crystals present; kidney stones or kidney damage
