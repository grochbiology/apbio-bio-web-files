Chapter 35-39 Short Answer Take Home Test

 

Directions: You may use your textbook and internet sources (all must be cited in your reference list), BUT you are not to discuss this test with anyone else.

 

1. List the characteristics of angiosperms (mono and dicots). Are these characteristics improvements over earlier land plants? Explain.

2. Distinguish between parenchymal, collenchymal and sclerenchymal tissues using diagrams. Give an example of each with a brief explanation of their importance.

3. Using the transpiration-cohesion-adhesion theory describe how xylem sap can be pulled upwards. Be very specific and use diagrams.

4. What are the nine macronutrients and seven micronutrients required for plant growth? Describe the importance of each.

5. Discuss the two functions of leghemoglobin (leghaemoglobin) and why is its synthesis evidence for coevolution?

6. Distinguish between; complete/incomplete, perfect/imperfect flowers and monoecious and dioecious plants.

7. Name the major classes of plant hormones, briefly describe their major functions and where the hormones are primarily produced.

8. Describe the importance of soil bacteria for land plants. Use one specific example.

9. Explain briefly  how plants respond to the environment; gravity, movement, stress, and  light ( include the action of phytochromes, photoperiodism (short-day, day-neutral, and long-day).

10. Explain the molecular basis of resistance to non-virulent and virulent pathogens.
