Unit 1 Study Guide: Cell Chemistry (updated 9/29/06) (Ch 1,2 and 17.2, journal notes, Cornell notes, warm-ups, labs)

There is no guarantee that all items are mentioned or will show up on the unit test.

1. Scientific Method, Independent, Dependent, Standardized (Controlled), Control Set-up, Hypothesis,

2. Qualitative vs. Quantitative Observations vs. Inferences

3.Atomic structure, protons, electrons, neutrons, mass number, valence electrons, isotope, ions, types of bonds ( in order of strength of bond attraction;  ionic (strongest), covalent , hydrogen (weakest)), energy stored in chemical bonds (valence electrons); solute + solvent = solution, why is carbon able to make so many different compounds?

4. pH (hydrogen ion concentration), pH scale (0-6 acid, 7 neutral, 8-14 base), example acid: HCl, base: NaOH. BTB (bromthymol blue indicator for carbon dioxide)

5. biomolecules (structures of carbohydrates, lipids, proteins), know the function and recognize monomers of each macromolecule (monosaccharide, nucleotides, amino acids), some dimers (sucrose, etc); polymers (polysaccharides- starch, glycogen; polypeptides, proteins; nucleic acids (RNA, DNA) how polymers are made or broken down via condensation (dehydration) and hydrolysis reaction. Lipids- two parts; glycerol and fatty acids (types of fatty acids: saturated, unsaturated, trans (how are they different?)

6.Know the chemical indicators for each of the biomolecules (Benedict's, Biurets, Sudan III/IV, cabbage juice, Lugol's (IKI))

7. Enzymes (mostly made of proteins, reuseable, substrates, effect of heat (temperature), salt concentration, pH on enzyme structure), structure of enzymes, function (how enzymes work, lock and key), energy diagrams (how are they set up what do they show, activation energy, endergonic, exergonic reactions), thermodynamics (1st and 2nd law); chemical reactions (reactants-substrates, products)

8. Early earth (how Earth formed, types of gases, condition of surface, oceans), requirements for life, first organisms; Miller/Urey Apparatus (purpose, set-up, findings)
