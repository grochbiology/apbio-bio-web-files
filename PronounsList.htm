Indefinite Pronouns:

do not refer to a specific person, place or thing

always singular: anybody, anyone, another, each, either, everybody,
everyone, nobody, no one, neither, one, other, someone, somebody

always plural: many, both, few, several, others

number plural , quantity singular: all, any, some, none

Demonstrative Pronouns: point out something definite

singular: this, that

plural:

these, those

* these words above are only pronouns when not followed by a noun. (* as per Amanda Swenson '09)

Interrogative Pronouns: used when asking questions

who, whose, whom, which, what

Relative Pronouns: introduce a clause

who, whose, whom, which, that, what, whoever, whomever, whatever, whichever

Contractions: it's: it is who's: who is, who has
