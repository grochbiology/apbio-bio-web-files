Cell PBL Scenario

Your Accelerated Biology teacher has been assigned to go to a week of staff development.  Since the Accelerated and the regular biology students are both studying the cell (at different learning levels) and knowing that substitute teachers generally don't have a science background, your teacher has gotten permission for her Accelerated students to teacher the regular students in her absence.  The substitute will be in the class to maintain discipline.

 

So that you are thoroughly conversant in the topic that you are expected to cover, your teacher has assigned you to develop and design the lesson that you will teach the regular students. You will also need to develop an assessment tool for evaluating your activity. Below you will find the key words  you will need to incorporate into the lesson. The group that comes up with the most creative and effective lesson will earn a full credit on the weekly quiz.       “ If you have to teach it, you really know it!”

 

Cell Membrane (plasma membrane) mitochondria (plural) chloroplasts endoplasmic reticulum (rough and smooth)
ribosomes nucleus/ nucleolus/ chromatin/ chromosomes Golgi bodies (Golgi apparatus) lysosomes
cytoskeleton (microtubules, microfilaments) vacuoles vesicles cell wall
cytoplasm (cytosol) whatever else...    

Assignment Requirements for 2 student partners:

1) Provide a "lecture" outline, what are you going to cover.

2) Provide the "lesson"... (example, worksheet, slideshow presentation, skit script....)

3) Provide the "assessment"... (example, quiz, game, worksheet...)
