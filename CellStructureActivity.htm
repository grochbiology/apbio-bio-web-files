Cell Structure Activity:

Scenario:  You work for "The Cell Company."  Our mission is to cell, cell, cell.  Your are a member of a department (the organelle or structure) within the "Cell Company."  Money is tight in this economy and you must justify why your department (the organelle or structure) is absolutely essential to the success of "The Cell Company." Do not forget to point out why your department CANNOT be cut out (cell or organism death).Failure to successfully defend the existence of your department (the organelle or structure) will result in layoffs (you’re out of a job). 

 

Directions:

  1. You and your lab partners will be assigned one or more organelles to research and present in class.
  2. You and your lab partners will have to justify the importance of your organelles or a structure to the parent organization of your “cell company”.
  3. Your group presentation must be appropriate to the task, clear, concise (5 minutes max.). Your group will use the  LCD projector and the SmartBoard (slide show, or other document must be emailed to the teacher in time for your presentation). You can use PowerPoint, create a YouTube video (with Windows Movie Maker or iMovie, do voice over with Audacity), create animations, etc. Just remember that the audio portion must be easily understood and the video portion can't be jumpy or difficult to make out, and all members of the group must be either visible or audible for full credit.
  4. Written requirements: a physical piece of paper with all members listed and all references used. (Format for references: Last Name, F. I. (Date). Title of book, or web site. Publisher or source of web page (ex. University of California). Pages or URL.)

 

Rubrics (grading scale).

50-45 points:  Clear, concise and convincing. Written copy and oral presentation excellent. Presentation emailed to teacher on time.

44-40 points:  Didn’t finish within 5 minutes time, all else good.

39-35:  Not all members involved, somewhat confusing, not completely convinced of importance of organelle or structure, presentation delayed teacher did not received copy of presentation on time.

34-30:  Disorganized oral and written presentation incomplete, presentation delayed.

29-0: Oral presentation done by one person only, written presentation incomplete, presentation delayed.

 Table 3: nucleus/nucleolus 

Table 4:  mitochondria/chloroplast

 Table 5:   Endoplasmic reticulum, smooth and rough

Table 6:    ribosomes/vacuoles          

Table 7: Golgi apparatus/lysosomes 

Table 8: cytoskeleton/cytoplasm/microtubles and microfilaments
