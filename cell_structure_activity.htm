Cell Structure Activity:  Sell that Cell

Scenario:  You work for "The Cell Company."  Our mission is to cell, cell, cell.  Your are a member of a department (the organelle or structure) within the "Cell Company."  Money is tight in this economy and you must justify why your department (the organelle or structure) is absolutely essential to the success of "The Cell Company." Failure to successfully defend the existence of your department (the organelle or structure) will result in layoffs (you’re out of a job). 

Directions:

  1. You and your lab partners will be assigned one or more organelles to research and present in class.
  2. You and your lab partners will have to justify the importance of your organelles or a structure to the parent organization of your “cell company”.
  3. Your group presentation must be: appropriate to the task, clear, and concise (5 minutes max.). You can use the overhead or the LCD projector (slide show, etc).
  4. Written requirements: a copy of your presentation with all members listed and all references used. (Format for references: Last Name, F. I. (Date). Title of book, or web site. Publisher or source of web page (ex. University of California). Pages or URL.)

Rubrics (grading scale).

5:  Clear, concise and convincing. Written copy and oral presentation excellent.

4:  Didn’t finish on time all else good.

3:  Not all members involved, somewhat confusing, not completely convinced of importance of organelle or structure.

2:  Disorganized oral and written presentation incomplete.

1:  Oral presentation done by one person only, written presentation incomplete.

You will be working in lab groups.  You will be given about 10 minutes in class at the beginning of the assignment and at the end of the assignment to meet/discuss your topic.

Topics:

Table 1:  nucleus/nucleolus 

Table 2:  mitochondria/chloroplast

 Table 3:  Endoplasmic reticulum, smooth and rough

Table 4:  ribosomes/vacuoles

 Table 5:  Golgi apparatus/lysosomes 

Table 6:  cytoskeleton/cytoplasm/microtubles and microfilaments            

Table 7: cell membrane/cell wall
