Lab Activity Osmosis and Onion Skin.

1. Prepare an onion slide as described on page 194 in your textbook. Use tap water for your wet mount.

2. Observe and draw a single cell, label the magnification, the size of the cell and the cell parts (cell wall, water vacuole, nucleus, remember there are no chloroplasts visible).

3. Replace the tap water with 25% NaCl (saltwater) using the directions from page 194 (use a small piece of paper towel to draw the new fluid under the coverslip). Observe and draw the cell now, include the same information as listed in instruction number 2.

4. Replace the saltwater with distilled water. Observe and draw now, include the same information as listed in instruction number 2.

Answer the following questions in your journal:

Explain what happened to the cell in each different solution.

Draw a diagram of the movement of water for each situation.

Explain why the plant cell doesn't burst when exposed to a hypotonic (low solute concentration) solution.
