Wheat Germ DNA Extraction (Flinn Scientific)

 

Introduction:  This is a DNA extraction and isolation activity using common household chemicals.  With dish soap, meat tenderizer, baking soda and alcohol using raw wheat germ as a source of DNA.

 

Materials:

2 grams raw wheat germ                       tap water

3 ml of Dawn liquid detergent               thermometer

2 grams Adolph's Meat tenderizer         100- 250  ml beaker

20 ml 95% EtOH                                   50 ml graduated cylinder

55 ˚C water bath                                  ice bath

1 M NaHCO3                                         10 ml pipette

Jumbo paper clip                                  balance

 

Procedure:

1.  Measure 45 ml of tap water into a beaker and place it in a warm water bath.  Allow water temperature to reach 55˚C, do not allow to exceed 60˚C.

2.  Sprinkle the 2 grams of wheat germ into the beaker and gently stir in 3 ml of detergent.  Allow this mixture to incubate in the 55˚C water bath for 5 minutes.

3.  Gently stir in 2 grams of the meat tenderizer and 5 ml of the

1M NaHCO3.  Incubate this mixture for 15-20 minutes in the water bath.

4.  While this is incubating, straighten the paper clip and form a small hook at one end.  Roughen the hook portion with a file or steel wool. Set aside.

5.  Transfer the beaker containing the wheat germ mixture to an ice bath for a few minutes to quickly cool it to room temperature.  Stir gently during this period.

6.  Using the 10 ml pipette, carefully layer the ice-cold alcohol over the wheat germ solution in the beaker.  Allow the alcohol to flow from the pipette with the pipette tip held against the inside surface of the beaker just above the liquid level.

7.  There will be a visible interface between the alcohol layer and  the wheat germ mixture layer.  A fibrous white precipitate should be evident at the interface.  This is DNA.  Use the paper clip hook, immersed to the depth of the interface to spool (wind) up the DNA fibers.  Use a slow, twirling motion to avoid breaking and separating the fibers.

 

Questions:

1.  What part of the wheat germ contained DNA?

2.  What was the purpose of the detergent? the meat tenderizer? 

3.  Why was it important to keep the temperature of the mixture at 55˚C?

4.  Which type of biomolecule was eliminated by heat?

5.  What is the purpose of the sodium bicarbonate?

6.  Why is the cold alcohol necessary?  Why did a precipitate form at the boundary of the two layers?  Make sure you discuss solubility.

7.  What does the ability to spool DNA tell you about its structure?
