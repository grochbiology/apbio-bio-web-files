INTRODUCTION

(adapted from http://int.cysd.k12.pa.us/GradClass/Hollinger/introduction.htm)

You are to choose a site within one of the eight biomes found on our planet. They include: Tundra, Taiga, Temperate Deciduous Forest, Grasslands, Desert, Tropical Rainforest, Temperate Rainforest, and the Marine biome.

 

Project Overview:

Give a brief description of the ecosystem site and biome you have chosen, including information on where (geographically) this biome is found on our planet. Please include a brief definition of your biome and an example ecosystem within this biome. This will serve as an introduction to your PowerPoint project. Each item listed below must be addressed on the slides and must include a photograph of the biome. (Please "burn" your final product on a CD-ROM to turn in when due.  Create an inviting travel guide, a "website" for ecotourists to visit, some way to entice others to visit your "world." There will be signups for specific biome sites many of which are national and international parks and reserves.  Be creative in how you present the information. I expect that there will be no duplications because there are quite a few different ecosystems within the individual biomes.

Specific Requirements:

Within this very large geographic area, you must examine ONE ecosystem. This ecosystem may be terrestrial or aquatic (exception - marine biome!) or both. The following information must be included:  Start with this site to find your specific biome http://www.worldwildlife.org/science/ecoregions/biomes.cfm

1. Choose a catchy title guaranteed to attract interest of the visitors.

2. Include a world map or continental map to illustrate the location of your site.

3. Provide some historical background (who, what, when, where, how... folklore, surprising facts)

4. (Several slides) Provide information about the physical features of your ecosystem. This includes the abiotic factors and how they affect the ecosystem (please include a description of the average monthly temperatures and precipitation (rainfall) If you can find a specific a climatogram that's great, otherwise you'll have to find the data and graph a climatogram yourselves)  *online help with climatograms  http://www.openlands.org/midewin/sampleclimat.pdf

http://earthobservatory.nasa.gov/Laboratory/Biome/ This site has biome and climatogram information pages

http://www.lalc.k12.ca.us/target/units/chaparral/pages/climatograms.html  This one has a "blank" you can use to build your own climatogram

http://www.weather.com (type in any city; then click on averages for temperature and rainfall; the site will give you a separate graph for rainfall and temperature)

5. (Several slides) Include information about the biotic factors and how they affect the ecosystem. This includes:

A. limiting factors - density-dependent and density-independent (what specifically is limited in YOUR ecosystem)

B.  organisms found in this ecosystem; be creative in presenting this information, make sure if you use a food web the arrows are pointing to the   mouth of the predator (ORGANISMS MUST BE NATIVE TO YOUR ASSIGNED ECOSYSTEM)

1. producers (4 minimum)

2. consumers - primary, secondary, tertiary, etc. (6 minimum)

        C.  food web of at least fifteen different organisms

        D.  predator-prey relationships within the ecosystem (2 examples)

        E. role of predation in population control (2 examples)

        F. energy (productivity) pyramid or biomass pyramid (HAS TO BE SPECIFIC TO YOUR ECOSYSTEM, NO GENERAL INFO)

        G.   population pyramid for one population in ecosystem (really hard to do, worth extra)

        H. succession - primary and secondary (what would happen and what organisms would be involved) (HAS TO BE SPECIFIC  TO YOUR ECOSYSTEM!!!)

6. Things to do, places to see, supplies to bring, clothing, costs, photo types, day trips, overnight, excursions, dangers

7. the impact of human activity on this ecosystem

8. prediction of what may/will happen to this ecosystem in the future

9. Glossary of terms specific to your site.

10. FAQs (THE TOP FIVE)

PRESENTATION CRITERIA  

1. PowerPoint must be on a CD-ROM

2. All slides must be readable (avoid too many flying effects, transitions, overlays, difficult to read backgrounds).

3. All participants must PARTICIPATE, divide up the presentation... limited to 15 minutes (grade drops 5% for each minute over 15 minutes).

4. Presentation should look like a "travelogue" (look it up) not a traditional report.

Project Points:

80 points total for CD-ROM

20 points total for presentation

20 points for participation (determined by group members)
