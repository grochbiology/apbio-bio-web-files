The Cystic Fibrosis Quandary

 

Jim and Jenna got married four years ago.  They decided to wait a while to have children, but now they are ready to start their family.  The problem they both face is that both of their families have a history of Cystic Fibrosis.  Although both Jim and Jenna are healthy, they are worried about having a child with CF.  They decided to go to a genetics counselor to get more information to help in making a choice of whether or not to have their own children or adopt. You and your partner are Jim and Jenna.  You must agree on a decision about whether or not to have children and you must justify the decision to your families.

 

 

Family History Form

Client Name:  Jenna Long                                              Date January 30, 2001

 

Parents:  Jenna’s mother is Faye and her father is Donnie.  Neither one has CF.  Fayes’ brother, Jenna’s Uncle Joe, had CF and died at age 17.  Donnie had one brother, Fred and one sister, Betty.  Betty was diagnosed with CF and died when she was 12 years old.  Fred has never married, but he does not have CF.

Grandparents:  Faye knows little about her parents as they died when she was very young.  Donnie’s mother and father (Jenna’s grandparents), Sue and Jack, were not afflicted with CF, but Jack’s sister Ruth died of CF when she was very young.

Great-grandparents:  Both of Jack’s parents, Phillip and Mary, had siblings that died very young, possibly of CF.

Siblings:  Jenna has two brothers, Chase and Seth.  Chase has CF and Seth does not.

 

Family History Form                 

Client Name: Jim Howard                                              Date: January 30, 2001

 Parents: Jim’s father Mike has no history of CF in his family.  His mother Carol has two sisters, Gayle and Jean.  None of them has CF.

 Grandparents: Carol’s father Maurice (Jim’s grandfather) has no history of CF in his family.  Carol’s mother Helen had a brother named Bill who died of CF before his 14th birthday.

Great-grandparents: Neither of Grandmother Helen’s parents, James or Rona had CF.

Siblings: Jim has two brothers, Matt and Robert.  Robert is married to Julie, and they have a baby daughter Sara, who was just diagnosed with CF. Neither Matt nor Robert has CF.

 

PBL Requirements:

1.        Draw two pedigrees for both families.

2.       Predict the probability of Jim’s and Jenna’s phenotypes and genotypes using Punnett squares.

3.       Predict the probability of Jim and Jenna having a child with CF.

4.       Describe the type/pattern of inheritance demonstrated in CF.

5.       Describe and rate the types of possible treatments or cures (existing and future) using genetic engineering.

 

Assessment:

1.        A cover letter explaining your position.

2.       PBL requirements 1-5.

3.  At least 2 references using appropriate format.
