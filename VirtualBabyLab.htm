Part B: Forming a sex cell (sperm or egg): Meiosis

 

  1. Your genetic code has two genes (alleles) for each trait. You may only pass ONE of the two alleles to your offspring. Your baby may inherit the first or the second allele based on the following rules:
    1. If you are homozygous (dominant or recessive) for any trait, you can only pass on one type of gene. (Examples: BB = B or bb = b)
    2. If you are heterozygous for any trait, you must determine which of the genes will go into your gamete. To do this, flip a coin. If it comes up “heads,” use the dominant gene. If it comes up “tails,” use the recessive gene.
  2. Using the above rules, enter the allele for each trait on your “Genetic Code Of Your Sex Cell” in Part C.
  3. Obtain a slip of colored paper to represent your egg (pink) or sperm (blue).  Copy your genetic code of your gamete onto the slip of paper. Then put give it a secret code and place your sex cell in the proper container (sperm goes into the “sperm bank” and eggs go into the “egg crate.”)
